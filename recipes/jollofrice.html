A Nigeria Jollofrice

Nigerian Jollof rice is a smoky, spicy and colourful dish, considered a national main dish and party favorite. It consist of long-grain parboiled rice cooked in a rich, savory base of tomatoes, tomato paste, onions, scotch bonnet peppers, and meat stock, seasoned with thyme, curry powder, and bay leaves

The cooking process involve, firstly frying some sliced onion in oil, followed by the pepper mix, adding broth and all the seasoning and allowing the rice to steam slowly until it absorbs all the liquid, often creating a slightly burnt, bottom layer that boosts the smoky flavor

Ingredients

  • Rice
  • Pepper base (fresh tomatoes, scotch bonnet, tatashe, onion)
  • Tomato paste
  • Vegetable oil
  • Seasonings (curry powder, thyme, garlic, ginger, bay leaves, seasoning cubes, salt)

Steps

  1. Wash your long grain rice until it clear from starch
  2. Blend all pepper mix
  3. Fry the pepper mix in hot vegetable oil
  4. Add seasonsing
  5. Add the broth
  6. Add rice, combine and allow to cook
  7. Cover the pot thightly with aluminium foil and cook for about 30-40 minute on medium heat
  8. Add butter and sliced onion (optional) and stir for complete flavour
  9. Serve with a chilled drink
